Rosemary Stuffed Pork Loin

Description

A stuffed Pork Loin filled with a mixture of pureed Garlic and Rosemary. Topped with crispy Pancetta and a White Wine sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 2-1/2 lb Pork
  • 6-8 Cloves of Garlic
  • 2 Springs of Fresh Rosemary
  • 1/2 Bunch of Italian Parsley
  • Olive Oil
  • 4 oz of Thinly Sliced Pancetta
  • 2 cups of White Wine
  • 1/2 cup of Water
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste

Preparation

  1. Using a food processor or by hand, puree the Garlic, Rosemary leaves, Parsley, 2 tbsp of Olive Oil, Salt, and Pepper until smooth.
  2. Slice the Pork Loin lengthwise on one side. Be sure not to cut all the way through.
  3. Season the inside of the pork with Salt and Pepper.
  4. Spread the Garlic puree mixture evenly all over the inside of the Pork Loin leaving a 1" border on one side.
  5. Roll up the Pork Loin and cover the top with Pancetta. Tie sturdy with kitchen twine and place in a roasting rack. Allow the pork to come to room temperature for approximately 15 minutes while you preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  6. After the oven is preheated, add the Water and White Wine to the bottom of the roasting dish.
  7. Cook for about 1 hour and 15 minutes or more, until the internal temperature of the Pork reaches 155 degrees F.
  8. Allow the Pork to rest for 15 minutes. Skim off the fat from the roasting pan and discard.
  9. To serve, slice the pork into thick slices and top with the juices from the Wine mixture. Enjoy!
